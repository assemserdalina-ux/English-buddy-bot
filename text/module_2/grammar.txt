Grammar focus: Past Simple vs Past Continuous (review)

1. Past Simple – finished actions in the past
Form:
regular: play → played, start → started
irregular: go → went, have → had, see → saw

Uses:
• completed actions: He won the race yesterday.
• actions in a finished time period: They trained hard last week.

2. Past Continuous – actions in progress at a moment in the past
Form:
was / were + verb-ing
• I was running.
• They were playing basketball.

Uses:
• background action: I was running when I fell.
• two actions at the same time: She was stretching while he was warming up.

3. Together
• Past Continuous + Past Simple:
  I was playing football when I hurt my ankle.

Time words:
yesterday, last week, two days ago, while, when
